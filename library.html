An eye-opener on the functioning of the tech-underworld. Snowden draws the line how far emotions and necessity can take you. Also, how technology won't spare you. Deep.

Takes simple, yet almighty algorithms and provides clear examples of why and how they work. Necessity is the mother of all algorithms.

The Feynman Lectures on Physics

Feynman * Leighton * Sands Now, anyone with internet access and a web browser can enjoy reading a high quality up-to-date copy of Feynman's legendary lectures. This edition has been designed for ease of reading on devices of any size or shape; text, figures and equations can all be zoomed without degradation.

https://www.feynmanlectures.caltech.edu/

Information, Physics, and Computation

Oxford University press, 2009 This is an introduction to a rich and rapidly evolving research field at the interface between statistical physics, theretical computer science discrete mathematics, and coding information theory. It should be accessible to graduate students an researchers without specific training in any of these three fields.

What every computer science major should know

Having emerged from engineering and mathematics, computer science programs take a resume-based approach to hiring off their graduates. A resume says nothing of a programmer's ability. Every computer science major should build a portfolio. A portfolio could be as simple as a personal blog, with a post for each project or accomplishment.

http://matt.might.net/articles/what-cs-majors-should-know/
